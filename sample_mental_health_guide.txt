MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT GUIDE

ANXIETY MANAGEMENT TECHNIQUES

1. Deep Breathing Exercises
   - Find a comfortable position
   - Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 counts
   - Hold your breath for 4 counts
   - Exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 counts
   - Repeat 5-10 times
   - This activates your parasympathetic nervous system and helps calm anxiety

2. Progressive Muscle Relaxation
   - Start with your toes and work up to your head
   - Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds
   - Release and feel the relaxation for 10 seconds
   - Move to the next muscle group
   - This helps reduce physical tension associated with anxiety

3. Grounding Techniques
   - 5-4-3-2-1 Method: Name 5 things you can see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, 1 thing you can taste
   - This helps bring your focus to the present moment

DEPRESSION COPING STRATEGIES

1. Behavioral Activation
   - Start with small, achievable activities
   - Create a daily schedule with basic tasks
   - Gradually increase activity level
   - Focus on activities that once brought joy

2. Cognitive Restructuring
   - Identify negative thought patterns
   - Challenge irrational thoughts
   - Replace with more balanced perspectives
   - Practice self-compassion

3. Social Connection
   - Reach out to trusted friends or family
   - Join support groups
   - Consider therapy or counseling
   - Don't isolate yourself

STRESS MANAGEMENT

1. Time Management
   - Prioritize tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix
   - Break large tasks into smaller steps
   - Set realistic deadlines
   - Learn to say no when necessary

2. Self-Care Practices
   - Maintain regular sleep schedule (7-9 hours)
   - Eat nutritious meals regularly
   - Exercise for at least 30 minutes daily
   - Practice hobbies and interests
   - Take regular breaks throughout the day

3. Mindfulness and Meditation
   - Start with 5-10 minutes daily
   - Focus on your breath
   - Observe thoughts without judgment
   - Use guided meditation apps if helpful

CRISIS RESOURCES

If you're experiencing thoughts of self-harm or suicide:
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-8255
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- Emergency Services: 911

Remember: It's okay to ask for help. Professional mental health support is available and can make a significant difference in your recovery.

HEALTHY LIFESTYLE HABITS

1. Sleep Hygiene
   - Maintain consistent sleep schedule
   - Create a relaxing bedtime routine
   - Avoid screens 1 hour before bed
   - Keep bedroom cool, dark, and quiet

2. Nutrition
   - Eat regular, balanced meals
   - Stay hydrated throughout the day
   - Limit caffeine and alcohol
   - Consider how food affects your mood

3. Physical Activity
   - Aim for 150 minutes of moderate exercise weekly
   - Choose activities you enjoy
   - Start small and build gradually
   - Exercise releases endorphins that improve mood

PROFESSIONAL HELP

When to seek professional help:
- Persistent symptoms lasting more than 2 weeks
- Difficulty functioning in daily life
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
- Substance use to cope with symptoms
- Relationship problems affecting mental health

Types of mental health professionals:
- Psychiatrists: Medical doctors who can prescribe medication
- Psychologists: Provide therapy and psychological assessment
- Licensed Clinical Social Workers: Provide therapy and case management
- Licensed Professional Counselors: Provide therapy and counseling

Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Mental health is just as important as physical health.
